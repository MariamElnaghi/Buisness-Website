    Instructions:
  • Feed your Cat regular and scheduled appointments (examples of cat food below)
    1. Sardine
    2. Chicken
    3. Kitten food (Do not feed to big Cats
    4. Wet food
    5. Dry food
    6. Tuna (As treats only)

      but some are toxic for them like Aloe Vera... For more examples of toxic plants
      visit: https://www.thespruce.com/houseplants-toxic-to-cats-4775359

  • Stay alert for any changes in your pet's actions
    1. How aoften they vomit(See if they vomit more than once a year throughout the year, if so, then contact your vet)
    2. Eating habits (Look out if they reject their food and if they do offer them their favorite food and if they reject it contact your vet, if they don't then they are just being picky. Also look out if they eat more recently)
    3. Weight changes in cats are always concerning,2ï»¿ whether it's gain or loss.
      Either might be a sign of an underlying health problem. Weight loss is
      more urgent in the short term whereas weight gain is usually more harmful over time.
      If you are not sure about your cat's weight, make a vet appointment for a
      check-up or at least a weight check.
    4. Check their litter box (Check how much times they use it and if decreases contact your vet)
    5. For more illness signs visit:
      https://www.thesprucepets.com/signs-your-cat-is-sick-4165142

  • Avoid picking up your pet when it doesnt want to be pickked up
    because it makes them get annoyed and they might sctratch
    or bite you in a way that might hurt you severely or the might wiggle off and get hurt

  • Always be sure to not leave your cat
    wet after a bath so it doesn't catch a cold

  • Brush your cat's fur

  • Do not stress your cat, causes of stress for bunnies:
    1. Older Cats or new ones
    2. New places / people

  • It is better if you don't use a harness for your pet and also avoid dressing it up
